The Mascot

The Anglephant

A deep-sea creature, part anglerfish, part elephant. It lives in the darkest places — where databases hide their secrets, where dead tuples accumulate in the dark, where tombstones outnumber the living.

Its bioluminescent brain-light cuts through the void. Around it, barely visible, the shapes of database horrors — all lurking just beyond the light, revealed one by one.

It is not frightening.
It is the thing that finds what is frightening.

Stephen Sears Smith wrote "Eliphant" in San Francisco, 1851. He was in a hurry.
Paul Odell Clark found it in 1954. We named it in 2026.

The Central Thesis

The Database Is Innocent

The database is always innocent. Every sin is a people problem. Every horror was a decision. Every creature was built by a human — with care, from correct parts, assembled wrong, and left to grow.

Assay finds the evidence. The people left it there.
